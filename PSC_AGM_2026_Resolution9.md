# Palestine Solidarity Campaign AGM 2026

## Motion 9: Examples of Anti-Palestinian Racism (APR)

**Status:** Passed by overwhelming vote on 14th February 2026  
**Proposed by:** Taalib Shamsuddin  
**Seconded by:** Cllr Suleman Sarwar

---

# START OF MOTION TEXT 

 
Anti-Palestinian Racism (APR) comprises actions, inactions, narratives, or structures that target Palestinians or deny their identity, history, and rights. It includes the targeting or penalisation of supporters.

This PSC AGM resolves to recognise these non-exhaustive examples of APR, consistent with accepted equality and human rights norms.


# ERASURE AND DENIAL OF ETHNIC OR INDIGENOUS IDENTITY

1. Using the phrase ‘a land without people for a people without a land’ to deny Palestinian indigeneity in historic Palestine. (harassment; interference with identity; dehumanisation)

2. Denying the Nakba. (harassment; restriction on expression; denialism)

3. Failing to acknowledge Palestinians as an indigenous people with a collective identity, belonging, and rights in historic Palestine. (direct discrimination; interference with identity)


# DEHUMANISATION CREATING A HOSTILE ENVIRONMENT

4. Erasing Palestinians’ human rights, equal dignity, and worth. (direct discrimination; dehumanisation; harassment; interference with identity)


# RACIAL STEREOTYPING, CRIMINALISATION AND DEFAMATIONS

5. Defaming Palestinians or their allies as inherently antisemitic, a terrorist threat or sympathisers, or opposed to democratic values. (direct discrimination; harassment; restriction on expression)

6. Justifying violence against Palestinians. (dehumanisation; direct discrimination; incitement to violence; failure to protect)


# EXCLUSION AND SUPPRESSION OF PALESTINIAN PARTICIPATION

7. Requiring Palestinians or supporters to denounce acts more vociferously or holding them collectively responsible for others’ acts. (direct discrimination; harassment; restriction on expression)

8. Excluding Palestinian perspectives, Palestinians, or their allies, or pressuring others to do so. (direct discrimination; harassment; restriction on participation)

9. Politicians and officials failing to engage with Palestinian victims of war crimes or racism while engaging with other racial groups. (indirect discrimination; failure to protect)


# UNEQUAL APPLICATION OF LEGAL, POLITICAL AND HUMANITARIAN STANDARDS

10. Denying, delaying, or undermining recognition of Palestine as a state while supporting recognition of Israel. (structural discrimination; impairment of collective rights; restriction on participation)

11. Calling for a safe and secure Israel but not for Palestinian safety and security. (direct discrimination; failure to protect; humanitarian neglect)

12. Denying Palestinian refugees and their descendants the right of return under international law. (direct discrimination; exclusion)

13. Not applying the same international law standards to Palestinians as to other groups. (direct discrimination; restriction on participation)


# SYSTEMIC AND INSTITUTIONALISED RACIAL DISCRIMINATION

14. The systems of apartheid imposed on Palestinians, supporting these systems, or denying or minimising their existence against the overwhelming evidence. (apartheid, apartheid denial, structural discrimination)

15. Promoting or excusing extremist settler ideology, including hate or violence towards Palestinians, discriminatory laws, or theft of Palestinian land and resources. (direct discrimination; structural violence and discrimination, dispossession/theft)

16. The Balfour Declaration, by privileging one group on a religious basis, caused lasting harm to indigenous Palestinians by denying their rights and enabling colonisation. (direct discrimination, dispossession)


# INTERSECTION WITH ISLAMOPHOBIA

17. Islamophobia and hatred targeting Palestinian Muslims, non-Muslim Palestinians perceived as Muslim, and other Muslims supporting Palestinian rights (direct discrimination; racialisation)


# TARGETING SUPPORTERS OF PALESTINIAN RIGHTS BY ASSOCIATION

18. Targeting, vilifying, or penalising individuals for expressing solidarity with Palestinians, including treating support for Palestinian rights as suspect, extremist, or antisemitic by association. (direct discrimination; harassment; restriction on expression; racialisation by association)
